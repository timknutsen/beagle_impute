Our lab combines large-scale computational analyses with experimental validation to understand genome evolution across the tree of life.

Genome 3D Structure & Whole Genome Duplication
Hi-C Chromatin Polyploidy

Whole-genome duplication (WGD) is a major driver of evolutionary novelty, yet how it rewires the three-dimensional organisation of the genome remains poorly understood.

We use Hi-C and other chromatin conformation capture techniques, combined with gene expression and epigenomics data, to characterise how TADs, compartments, and loop structures are established, maintained, or disrupted following WGD events in salmonid fish and other lineages.

Key questions include: How quickly is 3D genome organisation re-established after duplication? Are sub-genome asymmetries encoded in 3D? Can 3D organisation explain differences in gene expression divergence between homeologs?

Fungal Genome & Trait Evolution
Comparative genomics Secondary metabolism Phylogenomics

Fungi represent one of the most evolutionarily diverse kingdoms of life, with genomes that vary dramatically in size, repeat content, and gene complement. Our group uses comparative and population genomics to understand the forces driving this diversity.

We focus particularly on secondary metabolite gene clusters — genomic hotspots for innovation — and on how horizontal gene transfer, transposable element dynamics, and selective pressures shape the genomic landscape of key ecological and agricultural fungal groups.

Salmon Genetics, Physiology & Welfare
GWAS Aquaculture Imputation

Atlantic salmon is both an ecologically important species and the cornerstone of Norway's aquaculture industry. Our lab uses population genomics, GWAS, and genotype imputation to improve our understanding of the genomic basis of complex traits relevant to production, health, and welfare.

Projects include: identifying QTLs for disease resistance and thermal tolerance; developing imputation methods tailored to the duplicated salmon genome; and investigating how management practices affect genomic diversity in farmed populations.

The beagle_impute pipeline developed in this lab provides a Snakemake workflow for population-scale genotype imputation using BEAGLE.

Eukaryote Genome Diversity in Norway
Genome assembly Biodiversity Long-read sequencing

Norway's diverse ecosystems harbour an extraordinary range of eukaryotic organisms, many of which lack reference genome sequences. This project aims to generate high-quality chromosome-level genome assemblies for a broad sample of Norwegian eukaryotes — from plants and fungi to animals and protists.

Using long-read sequencing technologies (PacBio HiFi, Oxford Nanopore) and modern assembly tools, we are building a genomic reference library that will support comparative genomic studies, population genetics, and conservation biology across Norwegian biodiversity.
